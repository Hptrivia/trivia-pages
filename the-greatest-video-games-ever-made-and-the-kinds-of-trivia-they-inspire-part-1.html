The Greatest Video Games Ever Made and the Kinds of Trivia They Inspire — Part 1

Some games are remembered because they sold well. Others are remembered because they changed how people think about games entirely. The strongest all-time titles usually do both. They leave behind memorable characters, iconic missions, distinctive settings, major story turns, and enough detail to support far more than surface-level questions.

That is what makes them strong trivia themes too. A game becomes especially good for quizzes when players can remember not just the main plot, but the world, mechanics, factions, locations, side characters, and the moments that stayed with them long after they finished playing.

This first part looks at five major games that have earned a place in all-time rankings and that also create very strong quiz material.

1. Grand Theft Auto V

Grand Theft Auto V works so well in trivia because it combines a huge open world with three main protagonists who all feel distinct. Michael, Franklin, and Trevor each bring different relationships, missions, and personality-driven moments, which gives the game much more variety than a lot of other open-world titles.

Trivia for Grand Theft Auto V can focus on heists, side characters, map locations, businesses, vehicles, radio culture, strangers and freaks missions, and the differences between the three playable leads. It is a very strong theme because it supports easy questions for casual players and much deeper ones for fans who spent serious time in Los Santos.

2. Red Dead Redemption 2

Red Dead Redemption 2 is one of the clearest examples of a game becoming an all-time heavyweight through world-building and emotional storytelling. Arthur Morgan, Dutch van der Linde, John Marston, and the rest of the gang are memorable enough on their own, but the game also benefits from a huge setting full of camps, towns, strangers, rival gangs, and story missions that people remember in detail.

Trivia for Red Dead Redemption 2 can cover gang members, locations, horses, weapons, robberies, rival groups, camp life, mission names, and the game’s biggest story turns. It is especially strong for medium and hard questions because players who love it usually remember specific details far beyond the main plot.

3. Elden Ring

Elden Ring stands out because it combines the challenge and atmosphere of a Souls-style game with a much bigger world full of lore, regions, bosses, item descriptions, and cryptic story threads. It is the kind of game that creates obsessive recall in players who really got into it, even if the storytelling is less direct than in more cinematic games.

Trivia for Elden Ring can focus on bosses, regions, demigods, NPC questlines, weapons, spells, factions, Great Runes, and different endings. It is a particularly strong trivia theme because it supports both gameplay-based questions and lore-heavy questions, which gives you more than one way to build a quiz around it.

4. The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim

Skyrim has stayed relevant for so long because it gives players a world they can disappear into. Cities, guilds, dragons, Daedric quests, civil war politics, skills, races, and dungeons all contribute to the feeling that the game is bigger than a single storyline. That depth is exactly what gives it lasting quiz value.

Trivia for Skyrim can cover holds, jarls, factions, shouts, artifacts, quests, races, followers, locations, and major lore figures. It works well for trivia because even players who approached it differently still tend to remember the same major places, mechanics, and recurring themes.

5. The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt

The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt belongs on this kind of list because it combines strong character writing with deep world-building and a huge amount of quest content. Geralt, Ciri, Yennefer, Triss, the Bloody Baron, and many of the game’s side characters are memorable enough that fans often remember entire questlines, not just the broad plot.

Trivia for The Witcher 3 can focus on regions, monsters, contracts, signs, relationships, kingdoms, Gwent, expansions, and the outcomes of major choices. It is one of the strongest game trivia themes because it has both lore depth and a lot of playable detail, which makes the questions feel varied instead of repetitive.

What Game Trivia for These Titles Usually Has in Common

Even though these games are very different, the trivia they inspire usually shares the same strengths. The questions work because the games give players enough to remember: strong worlds, clear identities, major events, repeated systems, and characters who do not blur together.

That is what separates strong game trivia from weak game trivia. A game becomes quiz-friendly when it gives people more than a release year and a box cover. It needs memorable missions, bosses, locations, mechanics, lore, or story turns that actually stick.
