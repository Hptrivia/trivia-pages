The School Subjects That Make the Best Quiz Topics — Part 2

Some school subjects work well in quizzes because they are built around clear facts and categories. Others work because people recognize the ideas even if they do not remember learning them formally. That is what makes educational trivia more flexible than it first sounds.

This second part continues with five more school subjects that make strong quiz topics. These are subjects where questions can test memory, logic, reading, interpretation, or modern knowledge without becoming too dry.

6. Literature

Literature works well for trivia because it gives you authors, titles, characters, genres, famous lines, and big themes that people often encounter in school and beyond it. Even players who are not heavy readers usually recognize some major books, playwrights, or literary figures.

Trivia for literature can cover novels, authors, poems, plays, opening lines, fictional characters, literary periods, and book-to-screen adaptations. It is a strong topic because it can move between classic texts, modern books, and broad reading culture very naturally.

7. Mathematics

Mathematics can be a better quiz topic than people expect because it is built around patterns, numbers, formulas, and rules that many players still partly remember. While it can become too technical if written badly, it works well when the questions focus on concepts people can actually recognize.

Trivia for mathematics can cover shapes, formulas, number types, famous constants, percentages, probability, sequences, and historical mathematicians. It works especially well when the questions are short, clear, and based on familiar ideas rather than long calculations.

8. Astronomy

Astronomy is one of the most naturally engaging quiz subjects because people are already curious about space. Planets, moons, stars, black holes, telescopes, astronauts, constellations, and space missions all create memorable question material without needing a classroom context to feel interesting.

Trivia for astronomy can cover planets, solar system facts, constellations, famous missions, scientists, galaxies, moons, and basic space phenomena. It is especially strong because it feels educational and entertaining at the same time.

9. Computer Science

Computer science works increasingly well as a quiz topic because technology is now part of everyday life. Even people who never studied it formally still recognize ideas around coding, hardware, software, the internet, famous companies, and the history of computing.

Trivia for computer science can cover programming terms, major inventions, operating systems, internet basics, computing history, famous founders, hardware parts, and digital concepts people use every day. It is a strong modern quiz topic because it connects school-style knowledge to real life very directly.

10. Economics

Economics makes a strong quiz topic when the questions stay grounded in ideas people can understand rather than drifting too far into jargon. Markets, supply and demand, inflation, trade, money, incentives, and famous economists all give the subject clear quiz angles.

Trivia for economics can cover basic concepts, currencies, famous economists, financial institutions, economic terms, trade ideas, and modern examples of inflation or recession. It works best when the questions connect theory to the real world rather than sounding like an exam paper.

What These Subjects Add to Educational Trivia

This second set shows that educational quiz topics do not all need to rely on pure memory. Some, like literature and history, depend more on reading and recall. Others, like maths or economics, work better when they test recognition and understanding. Subjects like astronomy and computer science sit somewhere in the middle, where curiosity and modern relevance make the knowledge easier to enjoy.

That is why educational trivia can feel broader and more replayable than people expect. The strongest topics are the ones that already carry enough meaning, recognition, or usefulness that players feel rewarded for knowing them.

Put together, both parts show why some school subjects turn into much better quiz categories than others. The best ones are not just educational. They are the ones that remain memorable enough to still feel playable.
