The Greatest Video Games Ever Made and the Kinds of Trivia They Inspire — Part 2

The strongest game trivia themes usually come from games with depth. That depth can come from open-world exploration, memorable story choices, lore, side quests, factions, characters, or gameplay systems that stay in players’ heads long after they stop playing.

This second part continues with five more major games that create strong quiz material for different reasons. Some are remembered for emotional storytelling, others for scale, and others for the sheer amount of choice and detail they give the player.

6. Grand Theft Auto IV

Grand Theft Auto IV stands out because it feels more grounded and character-driven than some other entries in the series. Niko Bellic is one of the most recognizable protagonists Rockstar has created, and Liberty City gives the game a very specific identity built around crime, betrayal, ambition, and immigrant struggle.

Trivia for Grand Theft Auto IV can focus on story missions, family connections, friends and allies, borough-inspired locations, gangs, businesses, memorable quotes, and the game’s darker choices. It is a strong trivia theme because players who liked it usually remember its characters and tone very clearly.

7. Baldur’s Gate 3

Baldur’s Gate 3 became one of the defining modern RPGs because it gives players an unusual amount of freedom while still creating a cast and world people remember in detail. Companions, classes, races, romances, dialogue paths, and major story choices all matter, which makes the game naturally rich for quiz material.

Trivia for Baldur’s Gate 3 can cover companions, class mechanics, locations, factions, enemies, gods, artifacts, romance options, quest outcomes, and strange one-off encounters. It is especially strong because both gameplay systems and story choices can be turned into questions, which keeps the quiz varied.

8. Mass Effect 2

Mass Effect 2 is remembered as one of the strongest story-driven RPGs because of its squad structure, loyalty missions, and the sense that every major character actually matters. Commander Shepard is important, but so are Garrus, Tali, Miranda, Mordin, Thane, Legion, and the rest of the crew. That gives the game a very broad memory base.

Trivia for Mass Effect 2 can focus on squadmates, species, loyalty missions, ships, planets, organizations, choices, romances, and the final mission. It works especially well for character-based quizzes because fans often remember who was recruited when, what problems they had, and how their storylines developed.

9. The Last of Us

The Last of Us earns its place because it combines survival gameplay with one of the most talked-about character journeys in modern gaming. Joel and Ellie are at the center of that, but the game also leaves strong memory through locations, infected types, survivor groups, emotional set pieces, and dialogue that players do not easily forget.

Trivia for The Last of Us can cover characters, cities, factions, infected variants, weapons, key scenes, relationships, and the moral weight of the ending. It is a powerful trivia theme because fans usually remember not only what happened, but how it felt, which makes the details stick more strongly.

10. God of War (2018)

God of War (2018) works so well because it reinvented a major franchise without losing its identity. Kratos remained iconic, but the shift toward Norse mythology, a closer camera, more emotional storytelling, and the father-son relationship with Atreus gave the game a fresh kind of depth.

Trivia for God of War can focus on gods, realms, bosses, weapons, runes, companions, mythology, character development, and story progression. It is especially good for quizzes because it mixes lore-heavy material with very concrete gameplay details that players remember from combat and exploration.

What These Games Add to Game Trivia

This second set shows that game trivia does not only work through open worlds and giant maps. It can also work through relationships, moral choices, loyalty missions, mythology, emotional storytelling, and the feeling that the player’s decisions actually mattered.

That is why game trivia remains one of the strongest quiz categories. A good game leaves players with more than a vague memory of beating it once. It gives them characters, settings, enemies, choices, and systems that stay in their heads and can later be turned into strong quiz questions.

Taken together, both parts show why certain games keep showing up in all-time discussions and why they also make some of the best trivia themes. They give players enough detail to remember and enough variety to make the questions feel worth answering.
